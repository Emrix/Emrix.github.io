Christa McAuliffe Space Center

December 2020 - Present

The Christa McAuliffe Space Center (CMSC) aims to teach children about a broad range of topics such as social studies, teamwork, history, and a variety of science topics in a fun and engaging manner. They do this through a number of futuristic space flight simulators and a state-of-the-art planetarium.

I've had a long history at the CMSC, starting originally in 2010, and taking a break for college and an LDS Mission. I've been rehired and fulfill a number of roles there, the primary of which include a Planetarium Navigator (or presentor), and a Prop and Panel Creator. The following is a list of the more major activities that I have performed at the CMSC:

  • Planetarium Presentor - I present 50 minute shows on science topics (primarily space related) to the general public. I also present laser shows.
  • Planetarium Contect Creator - I create tools and snippets of content for use in the Planetarium.
  • Prop Creator - Using my skills in 3D Modeling, Wood working, electronics, and programming, I develop props for use during the Space Flight Simulations.
  • Panel Creator - Using my maker skills mentioned above I design and create dial and switch panels that get mounted on the wall of the Space Flight Simulations, and are used during simulations.
  • Engineering Department Head - The Space Center runs on volunteers. These volunteers are typically high school age. Under the direction of the Outreach Coordinator at the CMSC, I started a department that teaches these volunteers skills on how to make props and program different things for use in the space simulations.
  • Lobby Staff - During my shifts, I welcome guests, manage our gift shop, do general cleaning, and answer email and phone calls.
  • Intigator of Shenanigans - Most recently, I secured a number of nerf guns and darts, were myself and the other staff held a nerf war through the whole building
